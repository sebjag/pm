The high priest and his brothers did not view themselves as too important to share in rebuilding Jerusalem’s walls (Ne 3:1)
 
[Ne 3:1]

Some prominent men refused to “lower themselves” to help with the repair work (Ne 3:5; w06 2/1 10 ¶1)
 
[Ne 3:5]
5 And next to them the Te·koʹites+ did repair work, but their prominent men would not lower themselves to share in* (Footnote: Lit., “not bring their neck into.”) the service of their masters.

 
[w06 2/1 10 ¶1]
Highlights From the Book of Nehemiah

3:5, 27. We should not consider manual work done in the interests of true worship beneath our dignity, as did the “majestic ones” of the Tekoites. Rather, we can imitate the common Tekoites who willingly expended themselves.


 

God-fearing women willingly shared in the physically demanding project (Ne 3:12; w19.10 23 ¶11)
 
[Ne 3:12]
12 And next to them Shalʹlum the son of Hal·loʹhesh, a prince of half the district of Jerusalem, did repair work, along with his daughters.

 
[w19.10 23 ¶11]
What Will Jehovah Cause You to Become?

11. What did the daughters of Shallum do, and who today are imitating them?

11 Many centuries later, the daughters of Shallum were among those whom Jehovah used to share in repairing the walls of Jerusalem. (Neh. 2:20; 3:12) Although their father was a prince, Shallum’s daughters were willing to do that difficult and dangerous work. (Neh. 4:15-18) What a contrast to the prominent men of the Tekoites, who “would not lower themselves to share” in the work! (Neh. 3:5) Just imagine the joy that Shallum’s daughters felt when the project was completed in just 52 days! (Neh. 6:15) In our day, willing sisters are happy to help perform a special form of sacred service​—the construction and maintenance of buildings that are dedicated to Jehovah. Their skill, enthusiasm, and loyalty are vital to the success of this work.


 
 Many tasks that must be performed in the congregation involve manual or menial work and may occur behind the scenes.​—w04 8/1 18 ¶16.
 
[w04 8/1 18 ¶16]
Cultivating a Christlike View of Greatness

16 All Christians, young and old, should seek to cultivate the Christlike view of greatness. In the congregation, a variety of tasks must be performed. Never resent being asked to do things that may appear to be lowly. (1 Samuel 25:41; 2 Kings 3:11) Parents, do you encourage your children and teenagers to work cheerfully at any assignment that they are given to do, whether at the Kingdom Hall, at an assembly, or at a convention site? Do they see you perform lowly tasks? One brother, who now serves at the world headquarters of Jehovah’s Witnesses, vividly recalls his parents’ example. He said: “The way they treated the job of cleaning the Kingdom Hall or a convention center told me that they considered it to be important. They often volunteered to perform tasks that were for the good of the congregation or the brotherhood, no matter how menial those tasks might appear to be. This attitude has helped me to accept willingly any work assignment here at Bethel.”

[《守》2006/2/1刊9页1段]
尼希米记经文选讲

4:17,18 建造城墙的人怎么可能只用一只手工作？ 这对搬运工人来说不成问题。他们只要把物料放在头上或肩上，就不难一手扶稳物料，“一手拿着枪矛”。至于需要双手工作的建造工人，他们“建造城墙的时候”，每人“都束腰佩剑”，一旦敌人来袭，就可以随时反击。


 
[w06 2/1 9 ¶1]
Highlights From the Book of Nehemiah

4:17, 18​—How could a man do the work of rebuilding with just one hand? For burden bearers this would not be a problem. Once the load was placed on their head or shoulders, they could easily balance it with one hand “while the other hand was holding the missile.” The builders who needed both hands to do their work “were girded, each one with his sword upon his hip, while building.” They were ready to go into action in case of an enemy attack.

圣经说的真的能相信吗？

能，至少有两个理由：

  • 上帝痛恨苦难和不公正的事。在古代，崇拜上帝的人受到恶待时，耶和华上帝有什么感觉呢？圣经说，他看到他们“被人压迫欺凌”，就感到伤心难过。（士师记2:18）

    上帝憎恨坏人。例如，圣经说“喜爱暴力的人，他都憎恨”。（诗篇11:5）

  • 上帝关心我们每一个人。不是只有受苦的人“知道自己的忧伤和痛苦”，耶和华也清楚知道。（历代志下6:29,30）


     

    耶和华很快就会通过他的王国为我们每个人消除痛苦。（马太福音6:9,10）目前，上帝会让每个真心寻求他的人得到安慰。（使徒行传17:27；哥林多后书1:3,4）

靠辛劳的成果享福

1．有时候我们传道的热心可能会减退，为什么呢？

1 上帝赋予人类的一个自然愿望是“靠辛劳的成果享福”。（传2:24）可是，如果我们在传道工作上看来没有成果，就可能会灰心，失去喜乐，不像以往那么热心传道。我们可以怎样保持积极的态度呢？

2．为什么我们应该对传道工作有合乎现实的期望？

2 合乎现实的期望：请记住，虽然只有很少人响应耶稣传讲的真理，但耶稣确实完成了上帝交给他的任务。（约17:4）耶稣在撒种的比喻里提及几种不同的内心情况，他预告大部分人的内心情况最终都会阻碍他们接受由种子代表的王国信息。（太13:3-8,18-22）其实，我们辛勤努力传道是可以得着不少成果的。

3．即使传道时反应不理想，为什么我们仍然可以结出果实呢？

3 “结出果实”的真正含义：耶稣在比喻里指出，接受王国信息的人会“结出果实”。（太13:23）小麦萌芽成熟之后，就会结出果实，但果实不是一整株一整株的小麦，而是麦穗，是新种子。因此，基督徒所结的果实不一定是指新门徒，而是指王国的信息受到大大宣扬，结出更多倍的王国种子。他们所结的果实是指他们努力宣扬王国。不管别人对真理有什么反应，我们的辛劳也能带来满足和很多美好的成果，因为我们有份使耶和华的名彰显为圣。（赛43:10-12；太6:9）我们能够成为上帝的同工是莫大的殊荣，而我们“用嘴唇所献的果实”能使耶和华十分欢喜。（林前3:9；来13:15,16）

4．为什么说，我们传道的成果也许是我们不知道的？

4 此外，我们辛劳的成果也许是我们自己看不见的。耶稣在地上传道时，也许有些人曾听过他所传讲的真理，却在耶稣完成他地上传道的职务之后才成为门徒。同样，我们向人传道撒播王国种子时，种子也许没有即时在别人心里发芽生长。这个人却可能在后来接受了真理，而我们是不知道的。传道工作的确能带来很多美好的成果。因此，愿我们“不断多结果实”，表明我们是耶稣的门徒。（约15:8）

See Good for Your Hard Work

1. What could dampen our zeal for the ministry?

1 Man was designed to “see good because of his hard work.” (Eccl. 2:24) However, when we fail to see good results from our ministry, discouragement can rob us of joy and dampen our zeal. What can help us to maintain a positive attitude?

2. Why should we have realistic expectations about the response to our ministry?

2 Have Realistic Expectations: Remember that although those who responded to Jesus were few, his ministry was unquestionably a success. (John 17:4) In his illustration of the sower, Jesus foretold that most hearts would ultimately not be receptive to the seedlike Kingdom message. (Matt. 13:3-8, 18-22) Nonetheless, our diligent efforts still accomplish much.

3. How may we “bear fruit” even if we see little response to our preaching?

3 How We Bear Much Fruit: According to Jesus’ illustration, those who accept the message would “bear fruit.” (Matt. 13:23) After a wheat stalk sprouts and matures, the fruit it produces is not little wheat stalks, but new seed. So the fruitage that successful Christians produce is, not necessarily new disciples, but the many-fold duplication of Kingdom seed​—our expressions about the Kingdom. This results in much “good” from which we can obtain satisfaction, regardless of whether people respond. We are contributing to the sanctification of Jehovah’s name. (Isa. 43:10-12; Matt. 6:9) We are enjoying the privilege of being God’s fellow workers. (1 Cor. 3:9) And such “fruit of lips” delights Jehovah.​—Heb. 13:15, 16.

4. What results, unknown to us, may our ministry have?

4 In addition, our hard work may be having results that we do not see. It is possible that some who heard Jesus preach did not become his disciples until after he finished his earthly ministry. Similarly, Kingdom seed that we sow may not take root and grow in a person’s heart until later, and he may come into the truth without our knowing. Indeed, our ministry accomplishes a great deal of good. Therefore, may we “keep bearing much fruit” and prove ourselves Jesus’ disciples.​—John 15:8.

Picture description: 经文
 

箴言8:4,7


 

摘要：改变音量、音调、语速可以让你清晰地传达思想，打动听者的心。


 

要诀：

  • 变化音量 提高音量能强调重点或激励听者。朗读圣经中的审判信息时该提高音量。降低音量能产生期待感，也能传达恐惧或焦虑的情绪。


     
    Picture description: 实用建议
     

    Box content: 不要频频提高音量，这会让听者感觉受到责备。音量变化不要太夸张，以免对方把注意力放在你身上。

  • 变化音调 只要听起来自然，你可以提高音调来表现热情，或强调事物的程度，例如大小或远近。你也可以压低音调来表达哀伤或焦虑的情绪。

  • 变化语速 表达兴奋的情绪时可以加快语速；谈到主要论点时可以放慢语速。


     
    Picture description: 实用建议
     

    Box content: 不要突然加快语速，这会使听者受到惊吓。不要因为说得太快而口齿不清。

STUDY 10

Modulation


 
Picture description: Cited scripture
 

Proverbs 8:4, 7


 

SUMMARY: Convey ideas clearly and stir emotion by varying your volume, pitch, and pace.


 

HOW TO DO IT:

  • Vary your volume. Increase your volume to highlight main points and to motivate your listeners. Do the same when reading Scriptural judgments. Decrease your volume to build anticipation or to convey fear or anxiety.


     
    Picture description: Practical tip
     

    Box content: Do not increase your volume so often that your listeners feel scolded. Avoid drawing attention to yourself by being overly dramatic.

  • Vary your pitch. If appropriate in your language, raise your pitch to express enthusiasm or to indicate size or distance. Lower your pitch to express sorrow or anxiety.

  • Vary your pace. Speak more rapidly to convey excitement. Speak more slowly when stating important points.


     
    Picture description: Practical tip
     

    Box content: To avoid startling your listeners, do not change pace abruptly. Do not speak so rapidly that your diction suffers.

LESSON 52

Why Our Clothing and Appearance Matter

We all have preferences about how we want to dress and groom ourselves. If we follow a few simple Bible principles, we can not only express our personal taste but also please Jehovah. Let us consider some of those principles.

1. What principles should guide our choices in dress and grooming?

We should choose “appropriate dress, with modesty and soundness of mind,” and maintain a clean appearance that reflects our “devotion to God.” (1 Timothy 2:9, 10) Consider these four principles: (1) Our dress should be “appropriate.” As you have observed at congregation meetings, Jehovah’s people have different tastes, but our dress and hairstyles show respect for the God we worship. (2) Dressing “with modesty” means our appearance would not be sexually provocative, nor should it draw too much attention to ourselves. (3) We show “soundness of mind” when we do not adopt every fad of dress and grooming that comes along. (4) Our appearance should always reflect our “devotion to God,” indicating to others that we worship the true God.​—1 Corinthians 10:31.

2. How can our appearance affect our fellow believers?

While we have the freedom to choose what we wear, we should think of the effect our appearance might have on others. We try our best not to offend anyone but, rather, to “please [our] neighbor for his good, to build him up.”​—Read Romans 15:1, 2.

3. How can our appearance attract others to true worship?

While we try to dress appropriately on every occasion, we pay particular attention to our dress when we attend congregation meetings and share in the ministry. We do not want to detract from the important message we are taking to people. Instead, our appearance can make the truth attractive to others and “adorn the teaching of our Savior.”​—Titus 2:10.
