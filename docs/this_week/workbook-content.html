9 希西家有这么坏的父亲，本来很有可能长大后怨恨上帝。今天，有些人的遭遇虽然不像希西家的那么恶劣，但他们经历困难时，还是觉得自己有充分理由“迁怒于耶和华”，或对上帝的组织心存怨恨。（箴言19:3）还有些人相信，他们的家庭环境不良，导致他们学坏或犯下跟父母同样的错。（以西结书18:2,3）这些想法正确吗？

11．为什么后来希西家能成为一个好君王？

11 虽然希西家的父亲是很糟糕的犹大王，希西家长大后却成为数一数二的贤君。（请读列王纪下18:5,6）他选择摆脱父亲的坏影响，转而向其他的好榜样学习。那时期的先知有以赛亚、弥迦、何西阿。这些忠仆传达来自上帝的信息，希西家很努力学习，让耶和华的提醒和纠正深印在自己心里。所以，希西家努力修正过去他父亲作恶多端所造成的难题。他大刀阔斧，采取一连串大规模的行动，包括洁净圣殿、为人民献上赎罪祭、打碎各地的偶像。（历代志下29:1-11,18-24；31:1）有一次，亚述王西拿基立威胁要攻打耶路撒冷。希西家面对这么大的危机，还是显出勇气和信心。他信赖上帝会拯救他，并用言行勉励人民也要信赖上帝。（历代志下32:7,8）后来，希西家一度变得心高气傲，耶和华纠正他，他就谦卑自抑、衷诚悔改。（历代志下32:24-26）希西家没有让他悲惨的过去毁掉他后来的一生。他反而表明自己是耶和华的朋友，是值得我们学习的好榜样。

You Can Serve Jehovah Despite a Difficult Upbringing

Hezekiah’s father, King Ahaz, did detestable things (2Ch 28:1; w16.02 14 ¶8)
 
[2Ch 28:1]
28 Aʹhaz+ was 20 years old when he became king, and he reigned for 16 years in Jerusalem. He did not do what was right in Jehovah’s eyes as David his forefather had done.+

 
[w16.02 14 ¶8]
Imitate Jehovah’s Close Friends

8. Describe Hezekiah’s background.

8 Unlike Ruth, young Hezekiah was born into a nation that was dedicated to Jehovah. But not all Israelites lived up to that dedication. Hezekiah’s father, King Ahaz, is a case in point. That wicked man led the kingdom of Judah into idolatry, even desecrating Jehovah’s temple in Jerusalem. It is hard to imagine Hezekiah’s childhood, for some of his brothers met a horrible death, being burned alive as sacrifices to a false god!​—2 Ki. 16:2-4, 10-17; 2 Chron. 28:1-3.


 

Hezekiah chose to serve Jehovah despite his father’s bad example (2Ch 29:1-3; w16.02 14 ¶9-11)
 
[2Ch 29:1-3]
29 Hez·e·kiʹah+ became king at the age of 25, and he reigned for 29 years in Jerusalem. His mother’s name was A·biʹjah the daughter of Zech·a·riʹah.+ 2 He kept doing what was right in Jehovah’s eyes,+ just as David his forefather had done.+ 3 In the first year of his reign, in the first month, he opened the doors of the house of Jehovah and repaired them.+

 
[w16.02 14 ¶9-11]
Imitate Jehovah’s Close Friends

9, 10. (a) Why might Hezekiah easily have become bitter? (b) Why should we not become bitter against God? (c) Why should we not think that our background determines the type of person we will become?

9 Hezekiah might easily have grown up to be a bitter, angry man who turned against God. Others who have endured far less hardship have thought that they had valid reason to become “enraged against Jehovah” or embittered toward his organization. (Prov. 19:3) And some people are convinced that their unwholesome family background dooms them to lead a bad life, perhaps to repeat their parents’ mistakes. (Ezek. 18:2, 3) Are such notions correct?

10 Hezekiah’s life record gives us a resounding answer: Absolutely not! There is never a valid reason to become embittered against Jehovah, for he is not the source of the evils that befall people in this wicked world. (Job 34:10) True, parents can exert a strong influence on their children for good or for bad. (Prov. 22:6; Col. 3:21) But this does not mean that a person’s family background determines his course in life. On the contrary, Jehovah has given all of us a precious gift​—the ability to choose what we will do and who we will be. (Deut. 30:19) How did Hezekiah use that gift?


Picture description: A young man prepares to go to a meeting while his family relaxes
 
 
 

11. What made Hezekiah one of Judah’s best kings?

11 Although Hezekiah was the son of one of Judah’s worst kings, he grew up to be one of the very best. (Read 2 Kings 18:5, 6.) Yes, his father was a terrible influence, but there were other influences to choose from. Isaiah served as a prophet in those days, as did Micah and Hosea. We can imagine King Hezekiah deeply absorbed in the inspired declarations of such faithful men, allowing Jehovah’s counsel and correction to sink into his heart. So Hezekiah set about righting the terrible wrongs that his father had committed. He did so by cleansing the temple, atoning for the people’s sins, and destroying pagan idols in a vigorous and far-reaching campaign. (2 Chron. 29:1-11, 18-24; 31:1) When he faced daunting challenges, such as the threat of attack on Jerusalem by the Assyrian king Sennacherib, Hezekiah showed profound courage and faith. He relied on God for salvation and strengthened his people by word and example. (2 Chron. 32:7, 8) Later, when Hezekiah needed correction for showing a haughty attitude, he humbled himself and repented. (2 Chron. 32:24-26) Clearly, Hezekiah did not let his past ruin his present or deprive him of a future. Rather, he showed that he was Jehovah’s friend and a fine example to imitate.

拿单——忠于上帝，促进纯正的崇拜

拿单忠心事奉耶和华，很支持大卫建造世上第一座崇拜真神的圣殿。不过，当时拿单只是表达个人的想法，而不是奉耶和华的名发言。那天晚上，上帝指示拿单先知向大卫传达一个截然不同的信息：大卫不是建造圣殿的人，他的一个儿子会负起这个重任。虽然这样，拿单宣布上帝要跟大卫立约，使他的宝座“万世稳固”。（撒母耳记下7:4-16）

关于建造圣殿，拿单的意见跟上帝的旨意不一致。但他没有不满，反而谦卑地接受上帝的旨意，乐意遵行。如果上帝纠正我们，我们不是应该效法拿单吗？这件事之后拿单还是先知，显示上帝仍旧悦纳他。看来耶和华启示拿单和传达异象的使者迦得，指示大卫组织四千个乐师在圣殿里服务。（历代志上23:1-5；历代志下29:25）


 
[w12 2/15 24-25]
Nathan—Loyal Advocate of Pure Worship

As a faithful worshipper of Jehovah, Nathan enthusiastically endorsed David’s plan to construct the first permanent center of pure worship on earth. On that occasion, however, Nathan apparently expressed his own feelings instead of speaking in Jehovah’s name. That night, God instructed his prophet to take a different message to the king: David would not build Jehovah’s temple. The person to do so would be one of David’s sons. But Nathan announced that God was making a covenant with David to the effect that his throne would become “firmly established to time indefinite.”​—2 Sam. 7:4-16.

God’s will did not harmonize with Nathan’s judgment with respect to temple construction. Without murmuring, however, this humble prophet acquiesced to Jehovah’s purpose and cooperated with it. What a fine example to follow if God should correct us in some way! Nathan’s subsequent acts as a prophet show that he did not lose God’s favor. In fact, it appears that Jehovah inspired Nathan, together with Gad the visionary, to direct David in organizing 4,000 musicians in temple service.​—1 Chron. 23:1-5; 2 Chron. 29:25.

第10课

语调变化


 
Picture description: 经文
 

箴言8:4,7


 

摘要：改变音量、音调、语速可以让你清晰地传达思想，打动听者的心。


 

要诀：

  • 变化音量 提高音量能强调重点或激励听者。朗读圣经中的审判信息时该提高音量。降低音量能产生期待感，也能传达恐惧或焦虑的情绪。


     
    Picture description: 实用建议
     

    Box content: 不要频频提高音量，这会让听者感觉受到责备。音量变化不要太夸张，以免对方把注意力放在你身上。

  • 变化音调 只要听起来自然，你可以提高音调来表现热情，或强调事物的程度，例如大小或远近。你也可以压低音调来表达哀伤或焦虑的情绪。

  • 变化语速 表达兴奋的情绪时可以加快语速；谈到主要论点时可以放慢语速。


     
    Picture description: 实用建议
     

    Box content: 不要突然加快语速，这会使听者受到惊吓。不要因为说得太快而口齿不清。

你准备好受浸了吗？

你学习圣经以来，一定知道了不少关于耶和华的事。为了符合圣经的教导，你也可能在生活上做了一些改变。可是你也许因为某些原因，犹豫要不要献身给耶和华并且受浸。这一课会谈谈一些常见的原因，并看看你可以怎样克服障碍。

1．你需要有多少知识才能受浸？

你要受浸，就必须“确切认识真理”。（提摩太前书2:4）这不是说，你要懂得回答所有跟圣经有关的问题才能受浸。就算是受浸很久的基督徒也需要不断学习。（歌罗西书1:9,10）不过，你还是要对圣经的道理有基本的了解。会众的长老会帮助你知道自己是不是已经做到了这点。

2．你要采取哪些步骤才能受浸？

受浸前，你必须“悔改”，并且“回头”。（请读使徒行传3:19）这意味着你要对以往做过的错事深感懊悔，并且求耶和华宽恕你。你也要坚决不做违背圣经的事，决心按照上帝喜欢的方式生活。另外，你也要参加会众的聚会并跟会众一起传道。

3．为什么你不用害怕踏出受浸这一步？

有些人怕自己不能履行对耶和华的承诺。老实说，每个人都有可能犯错，就连圣经里忠心崇拜上帝的人也不例外。不过，耶和华没有要求崇拜他的人十全十美。（请读诗篇103:13,14）只要你尽力而为，他就会很高兴，也会帮助你。事实上，耶和华向我们保证，什么都不能隔绝他对我们的爱。（请读罗马书8:38,39）

LESSON 47

Are You Ready to Get Baptized?

You have learned many things about Jehovah through your study of the Bible. Likely you made some changes to apply what you learned. Yet, there may be something holding you back from dedicating yourself to Jehovah and getting baptized. This lesson will discuss some common obstacles to baptism and how you can overcome them.

1. How much do you need to know before you get baptized?

To get baptized, you need to have “an accurate knowledge of truth.” (1 Timothy 2:4) This does not mean that you have to know the answer to every Bible question before you get baptized. Even Christians who have been baptized for a long time keep learning. (Colossians 1:9, 10) But you do need to have a basic understanding of Bible teachings. Congregation elders will help you find out if you have enough knowledge.

2. What are some steps you need to take before baptism?

Before you can get baptized, you must “repent . . . and turn around.” (Read Acts 3:19.) This means that you feel deeply sorry for any sins you have committed and you ask Jehovah to forgive you. You also firmly reject bad conduct and are determined to live in a way that pleases God. In addition, you begin to participate in congregation activities by attending meetings and preaching as an unbaptized publisher.

3. Why should you not let fear hold you back?

Some are afraid that they will not be able to do what they promised Jehovah. True, at times you will make mistakes, even as faithful men and women mentioned in the Bible did. But Jehovah does not expect his worshippers to be perfect. (Read Psalm 103:13, 14.) He is pleased when you do your best! He will help you. In fact, Jehovah assures us that nothing “will be able to separate us from [his] love.”​—Read Romans 8:38, 39.

我们必须受耶和华教导


1．来认识耶和华，接受他的教导，
圣经说快来喝生命之水！
上帝已赐下健全的教诲，

学习正义、领受上帝教诲。
有上帝力量和弟兄情谊，
我们步伐坚定，走在光里。
3．颂赞上帝之声多么鼓舞人心，
受他教导的人言词动听。
上帝的仆人要经常聚集，
团结一致敬奉伟大上帝。
