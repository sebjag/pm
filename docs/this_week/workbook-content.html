1．世人迷失在这混乱世界中，
不知道上帝的要求。
我们需要指引才能向前走，
单靠自己无法成功。

约伯称颂耶和华的名

17 约伯为什么能坚守忠义呢？显然，在还没遇到患难之前，他早已跟耶和华建立了亲密的关系。没有任何证据显示，约伯知道撒但曾质疑耶和华。不管怎样，约伯都已立定心意要忠于上帝。他说：“我到死也不会舍弃忠义！”（约伯记27:5）约伯为什么能跟耶和华建立这么紧密的关系呢？毫无疑问，约伯一定听过自己的远房亲戚亚伯拉罕、以撒和雅各怎样蒙耶和华恩待，他一定很用心细想这些事。此外，约伯观察耶和华所创造的一切，也可以对耶和华的特质有很深的认识。（请读约伯记12:7-9,13,16）

10．为什么跟忠贞的弟兄姐妹建立友谊非常重要？

10 跟忠贞的弟兄姐妹建立友谊。要跟会众里一些值得学习的弟兄姐妹做朋友，就算他们的年纪和背景跟你不一样，你也可以跟他们培养友谊。圣经说，“上了年纪的人”是有智慧的。（约伯记12:12）另一方面，我们也可以从一些比我们年轻的忠贞基督徒身上学到很多。大卫跟约拿单的年纪相差很大，但他们还是成了非常亲密的朋友。（撒母耳记上18:1）他们虽然遇到很多困难，却互相帮助，并肩崇拜耶和华。（撒母耳记上23:16-18）伊琳娜是家里唯一的耶和华见证人，她说：“会众里的人就像我们的父母和兄弟姐妹一样，耶和华会通过他们来照顾我们。”


 
 

11．你必须怎么做，才能跟别人建立牢固的友谊？

11 结交新朋友有时不容易，如果我们性格比较害羞就更是这样。生性内向的拉特娜姐妹虽然遭到反对，但还是接受了真理。她说：“后来我真的看出，没有弟兄姐妹的帮助是不行的。”也许你觉得很难把自己的感受告诉别人，但坦诚的沟通是建立亲密友谊的基础。弟兄姐妹很想鼓励和帮助你，但他们要了解你的需要，才能帮得上忙。


 
 

12．我们怎样做，就能结交到好朋友？

12 跟弟兄姐妹一起传道是建立友谊的好方法。上文提过的卡萝尔说：“我跟很多姐妹一起参与传道和其他崇拜活动，结果成了很好的朋友。这些年来，耶和华一直通过这些好朋友帮助我。”跟忠贞的弟兄姐妹交朋友确实很值得。耶和华会通过这些朋友来帮助你克服负面的情绪，比如孤单的感觉。（箴言17:17）

Three Ways to Gain Wisdom and Benefit From It

Observe creation (Job 12:7-9; w09 4/15 6 ¶17)
 
[Job 12:7-9]
 7 However, ask, please, the animals, and they will instruct you;

Also the birds of the heavens, and they will tell you.

 8 Or give consideration* (Footnote: Or possibly, “speak.”) to the earth, and it will instruct you;

And the fish of the sea will declare it to you.

 9 Who among all these does not know

That the hand of Jehovah has done this?


 
[w09 4/15 6 ¶17]
Job Held High the Name of Jehovah

17 What helped Job to maintain his integrity? Clearly, before the calamities struck, he had already cultivated a close relationship with Jehovah. Although we have no evidence that he knew that Satan had challenged Jehovah, Job was determined to stay loyal. He said: “Until I expire I shall not take away my integrity from myself!” (Job 27:5) How did Job develop this close relationship? No doubt, he cherished what he had heard about God’s dealings with Abraham, Isaac, and Jacob, who were his distant relatives. And by observing creation, Job could discern many of Jehovah’s qualities.​—Read Job 12:7-9, 13, 16.


 

Build friendships with faithful Christians (Job 12:12; w21.06 10 ¶10-12)
 
[Job 12:12]
12 Is not wisdom found among the aged,+

And does not understanding come with a long life?


 
[w21.06 10 ¶10-12]
With Jehovah, You Are Never Alone

10. Why is it important to build friendships with faithful Christians?

10 Build friendships with faithful Christians. Seek out friends in the congregation whom you can learn from, even those whose age or background is different from yours. The Bible reminds us that wisdom is “found among the aged.” (Job 12:12) Older ones can learn much from faithful younger ones too. David was much younger than Jonathan, but that did not prevent them from enjoying a close friendship. (1 Sam. 18:1) David and Jonathan helped each other to serve Jehovah despite serious challenges. (1 Sam. 23:16-18) “Our brothers and sisters can really become our spiritual parents or siblings,” says Irina, a sister who is currently the only Witness in her family. “Jehovah can use them to fill our need.”


 
 

11. What must you do to build strong friendships?

11 Making new friends may not be easy, especially if you are shy. Ratna, a timid sister who learned the truth despite opposition, admits, “I had to accept the fact that I needed the help and support of my spiritual family.” It can be a challenge to share your feelings with someone else, but such open communication is the basis for a close friendship. Your friends want to encourage and support you, yet they need you to let them know how they can do so.


 
 

12. How can the ministry help you to make good friends?

12 One of the best ways to make friends is by sharing in the ministry with fellow Christians. Carol, quoted earlier, says: “I have gained many good friendships by spending time with sisters in the ministry and in other theocratic activities. Jehovah has supported me over the years by means of these friends.” Building friendships with faithful Christians is definitely worth the effort. Jehovah uses such friendships to help you battle distressing feelings, such as loneliness.​—Prov. 17:17.


 

Learn God’s standards, and apply them in your life (Job 12:16; it-2 1190 ¶2)
 
[Job 12:16]
16 With him there are strength and practical wisdom;+

To him belong the one going astray and the one leading astray;


 
[it-2 1190 ¶2]
Wisdom

Divine Wisdom. Wisdom in the absolute sense is found in Jehovah God, who is “wise alone” in this sense. (Ro 16:27; Re 7:12) Knowledge is acquaintance with fact, and since Jehovah is the Creator, who is “from time indefinite to time indefinite” (Ps 90:1, 2), he knows all there is to know about the universe, its composition and contents, its history till now. The physical laws, cycles, and standards upon which men rely in their research and invention, and without which they would be helpless and have nothing stable upon which to build, are all of His making. (Job 38:34-38; Ps 104:24; Pr 3:19; Jer 10:12, 13) Logically, his moral standards are even more vital for stability, sound judgment, and successful human living. (De 32:4-6; see JEHOVAH [A God of moral standards].) There is nothing beyond his understanding. (Isa 40:13, 14) Though he may allow things that are contrary to his righteous standards to develop and even temporarily prosper, the future ultimately rests with him and will conform precisely to his will, and the things spoken by him “will have certain success.”​—Isa 55:8-11; 46:9-11.

父母该怎样跟青春期的孩子沟通

 

“我听得出言外之意吗？”约伯记12:11说：“正如舌头品尝食物，耳朵辨别话语。”（《现代中文译本修订版》）孩子处于青春期时，你特别须要了解孩子所说的话的含意。青少年常常把话说得很重。比如说，孩子可能说：“你总是 把我当做小孩子！”或者“你从不 听我说话！”你不必跟孩子争辩他的话对还是不对，要明白孩子所说的，很可能不是他真正想表达的意思。例如，“你总是把我当做小孩子”的意思可能是“我觉得你不信任我”，至于“你从不听我说话”，意思可能是“我只是想告诉你我的感觉”。因此，要尽量听出孩子的言外之意。


 
[w08 8/1 11 ¶5]
Communicating With Adolescents

 

‘Do I discern the message behind the words?’ Job 12:11 says: “Does not the ear itself test out words as the palate tastes food?” Now more than ever, you need to “test out” what your son or daughter says. Adolescents often speak in absolutes. For example, your son or daughter might say, “You always treat me like a child!” or “You never listen to me!” Rather than picking on the technical inaccuracies of “always” and “never,” recognize that your child is probably not speaking in literal terms. For instance, “You always treat me like a child” could mean “I feel that you don’t trust me,” and “You never listen to me” might mean “I want to tell you how I really feel.” Try to discern the message behind the words.

上帝的王国将要成就什么事？

虽然耶稣已经开始在天上统治，但地球仍然受人类政府管辖。不过，上帝已吩咐耶稣：“你要去敌人当中征服他们。”（诗篇110:2）在不久的将来，耶稣就会铲除所有敌对势力，并为愿意服从上帝的人带来纾解。

上帝的王国很快就会采取什么行动呢？

  • 毁灭错误宗教 许多宗教教人错误的道理，令人误解上帝，也使人活得很痛苦。圣经把这些错误宗教比作“娼妓”，并预告她会被铲除，而许多人会因为她的灭亡感到震惊。（启示录17:15,16）

  • 终结人类的统治 上帝的王国会采取行动，终结人类的统治。（启示录19:15,17,18）

耶稣预告会有哪些特定事件发生？

耶稣说：“民族要攻打民族，国家要攻打国家，还会有大地震，一处接一处有粮荒和瘟疫。”（路加福音21:10,11）就像手指的不同纹路构成指纹，耶稣预告的特定事件也共同构成一个非常明显的征象。凭着指纹，我们能辨认一个人的身份。同样，这些事件在同一时间发生时，我们就能看出“上帝的王国近了”。那么，这些事件都已同时发生，而且在世界各地都清楚可见吗？让我们来看看一些证据。


 

1. 战争


 
Picture description: 战斗机、坦克和导弹
 

1914年，一场战争爆发了，规模之大史无前例。很多历史学家都把1914年称为人类历史的一个转折点，因为在那一年，全世界首次卷入了同一场大战。不仅如此，人类大规模使用坦克、机关枪、空投炸弹、毒气及其他致命武器，也是从第一次世界大战开始的。在随后爆发的第二次世界大战中，人类发明了核武器并首次在战争中使用。1914年以来，世上的战火从未平息，夺去了亿万人的性命。


 

2. 地震


 
Picture description: 一场地震导致房屋毁坏
 

《大英百科全书线上教育版》指出，每年有差不多100场足以造成严重破坏的地震。根据1900年左右开始的观测纪录，美国地质测量局推断每年都会有大约16场大地震发生。有些人可能觉得，现在地震的次数看来明显增加，是因为探测技术提高了。不过，全球的大地震造成了前所未见的伤亡和损害，这是无可否认的事实。

11 耶稣从死里复活后向门徒显现，并交托给他们一项重任，说：“你们要去，使所有国族的人做我的门徒，奉天父、儿子、神圣力量的名为他们施浸，我吩咐你们的一切，都要教导他们遵守。看哪！在这一切日子，直到这个制度的末期，我都与你们同在。”（马太福音28:19,20）“我都与你们同在”这句话意味着，在传道和帮助人成为基督徒的工作上，门徒会得到耶稣的支持。他们实在需要耶稣的支持，因为他们会“被所有国族憎恨”，像耶稣曾预告的一样。（马太福音24:9）门徒也会得到另一项帮助。耶稣即将升天时告诉门徒，他们会从神圣力量获得力量，为他作见证“一直到地上最遥远的地方”。（使徒行传1:8）

12．哪些重要的问题值得想想？为什么我们必须知道答案？


 

12 有些很重要的问题值得想想：在1世纪，耶稣的使徒和门徒重视这项任务吗？当时这群人数不多的基督徒，是不是即使遭到残酷的迫害，也为上帝的王国作彻底的见证呢？在帮助人成为基督门徒的工作上，他们真的得到来自天上的支持和耶和华的神圣力量的帮助吗？只要读读使徒行传，就能找到上述及有关问题的答案。我们必须知道答案，为什么呢？因为耶稣曾指出，他所交托的工作会继续下去，“直到这个制度的末期”。换句话说，真正的基督徒都应该执行这项工作，我们这些生活在终结时期的基督徒当然也不例外。因此，我们应该认真查考使徒行传的记载。


 

使徒行传概要

13,14．（甲）使徒行传的执笔者是谁？他向谁搜集资料？（乙）使徒行传包含什么内容？


 

13 使徒行传的执笔者是谁呢？这卷书本身并没有直接说谁是执笔者。可是，使徒行传开头的话清楚显示，这卷书的执笔者跟路加福音的是同一个人。（路加福音1:1-4；使徒行传1:1,2）因此，路加这位“亲爱的医生”兼严谨的历史家，一向被公认为使徒行传的执笔者。（歌罗西书4:14）使徒行传涵盖的时期大概是28年，从公元33年耶稣升天开始，到大约公元61年使徒保罗在罗马获释为止。在使徒行传里，路加除了用“他们”这个代词，也不时用“我们”这个代词，可见在所记的许多事件中，路加本人也在场。（使徒行传16:8-10；20:5；27:1）路加在记事方面向来细心谨慎，想必他是直接向保罗、巴拿巴、腓力和其他当事人搜集资料后，才把事情记下来的。


 

14 使徒行传包含什么内容呢？在较早写成的路加福音中，路加主要记述耶稣的事迹；在使徒行传中，他主要记述耶稣门徒的事迹。换句话说，使徒行传着重谈论基督徒和他们所从事的非凡工作，尽管他们在别人眼中只是“没有受过教育的普通人”。（使徒行传4:13）简单来说，这卷受上帝启示的经卷讲述基督徒会众怎样建立起来，怎样日益兴旺。使徒行传也表明1世纪的基督徒对传道工作有什么态度，以及运用哪些方法执行这项工作。（使徒行传4:31；5:42）这卷书还指出，在传讲王国好消息方面，神圣力量发挥什么重要的作用。（使徒行传8:29,39,40；13:1-3；16:6；18:24,25）使徒行传强调圣经的主题——上帝会通过基督统治的王国彰显自己的圣名。这卷书表明，尽管遭受猛烈的反对，基督徒在传讲王国信息方面却节节胜利。（使徒行传8:12；19:8；28:30,31）

15．查考使徒行传的记载有什么益处？


 

15 查考使徒行传的记载确实能够巩固信心，令人振奋！我们细想早期基督徒的热心和勇气，必定会深受鼓舞。我们会受到激励去效法1世纪的弟兄姐妹，决心像他们一样信心坚定。这样，我们就能更好地完成使命，帮助人成为基督的门徒。你手上这本书就是要帮助你深入分析使徒行传的记载。


 
[bt chap. 1 ¶8-15]
“Go . . . and Make Disciples”

 

8 Are you, dear reader, among the throngs of Kingdom proclaimers who are now active in more than 235 lands? If so, you play a part in the thrilling expansion of the Kingdom-preaching work! What has been accomplished in the worldwide field is nothing short of miraculous. In spite of formidable obstacles and challenges​—even governmental bans and outright persecution—​Jehovah’s Witnesses are giving a thorough witness about God’s Kingdom to people of all nations.


 

9 An intriguing question to consider is, Why has no obstacle, not even satanic opposition, been able to stop the forward movement of the Kingdom-preaching work? To answer that question, we need to look back to the first century C.E. After all, we modern-day Witnesses of Jehovah are continuing the work that started back then.


 

A Far-Reaching Commission

10. To what did Jesus devote himself, and what did he know about this work?


 

10 The Founder of the Christian congregation, Jesus Christ, devoted himself to the preaching of the good news of God’s Kingdom; it was his lifework. He once explained: “I must also declare the good news of the Kingdom of God to other cities, because for this I was sent.” (Luke 4:43) Jesus knew that he was initiating a work that he could not complete all by himself. Shortly before his death, he foretold that the Kingdom message would be preached “in all the nations.” (Mark 13:10) How, though, would this be done, and by whom?


Picture description: Jesus speaking to a crowd of his disciples.
 

11. What weighty commission did Jesus give his disciples, and what support would they have in carrying it out?


 

11 Following his death and resurrection, Jesus appeared to his disciples and gave them this weighty commission: “Go, therefore, and make disciples of people of all the nations, baptizing them in the name of the Father and of the Son and of the holy spirit, teaching them to observe all the things I have commanded you. And look! I am with you all the days until the conclusion of the system of things.” (Matt. 28:19, 20) The words “I am with you” indicated that the disciples would have his backing in the preaching and disciple-making work. They would need such support, for Jesus had foretold that they would be “hated by all the nations.” (Matt. 24:9) The disciples could count on something else for support. Just before ascending to heaven, Jesus told them that they would be empowered by holy spirit to be his witnesses “to the most distant part of the earth.”​—Acts 1:8.

12. What important questions arise, and why is it vital that we know the answers?


 

12 Now some important questions arise: Did Jesus’ apostles and the other first-century disciples take their commission seriously? Did this relatively small band of Christian men and women bear thorough witness about the Kingdom of God even in the face of vicious persecution? Did they really have heavenly backing and the support of Jehovah’s holy spirit in their disciple-making work? These and related questions are answered in the Bible book of Acts. It is vital that we know the answers. Why? Jesus promised that the work he commissioned would continue “until the conclusion of the system of things.” So this commission applies to all true Christians, including those of us living in this time of the end. We therefore have a keen interest in the historical record contained in the book of Acts.


 

Overview of the Book of Acts

13, 14. (a) Who wrote the book of Acts, and how did the writer obtain his information? (b) What does the book of Acts contain?


 

13 Who wrote the book of Acts? The book itself never names its writer, but the opening words make it clear that the writer of Acts was also the writer of the Gospel of Luke. (Luke 1:1-4; Acts 1:1, 2) Thus, from early times, Luke, a “beloved physician” and a careful historian, has been held to be the writer of Acts. (Col. 4:14) The book covers a period of about 28 years, from Jesus’ ascension in 33 C.E. to the apostle Paul’s imprisonment in Rome about 61 C.E. The fact that Luke changes from “they” to “we” in his narrative suggests that he was present at many of the events that he describes. (Acts 16:8-10; 20:5; 27:1) A meticulous researcher, Luke no doubt obtained information firsthand from Paul, Barnabas, Philip, and others mentioned in the record.


 

14 What does the book of Acts contain? Earlier, in his Gospel, Luke wrote about the things that Jesus said and did. In the book of Acts, however, Luke reports what Jesus’ followers said and did. Acts, then, is about people who accomplished an extraordinary work, although many of them were viewed by outsiders as “uneducated and ordinary.” (Acts 4:13) In brief, the inspired record tells us how the Christian congregation was founded and how it grew. Acts shows how the first-century Christians preached​—their methods and their attitude. (Acts 4:31; 5:42) It highlights the role of holy spirit in spreading the good news. (Acts 8:29, 39, 40; 13:1-3; 16:6; 18:24, 25) Acts picks up the Bible’s theme, which involves the sanctification of God’s name by means of his Kingdom under Christ, and shows the triumphant spread of the Kingdom message in the face of fierce opposition.​—Acts 8:12; 19:8; 28:30, 31.

15. In what ways will we benefit from examining the book of Acts?


 

15 Indeed, it is thrilling and faith-strengthening to examine the Bible book of Acts! If we reflect on the bold and zealous example of Christ’s early followers, our hearts will be touched. We will be moved to imitate the faith of our first-century counterparts. We will thus be better equipped to fulfill our commission to “go . . . and make disciples.” The publication that you are now reading is designed to help you make a careful study of the book of Acts.

    我的依靠、希望和力量


    1．耶和华你赐予的希望，
    我们紧握不放。
    渴望与所有人分享，
    未来美好盼望。
    但有时忧虑让人心慌，
    喜乐被恐惧隐藏。
    希望曾经照亮心房，
    如今黯淡无光。
    你是我的依靠、希望和力量，
    我的需要你不遗忘。
    满怀信心将真理向人宣扬，
    因你时刻在我身旁。
    2．耶和华当我陷入困境，
    求你使我想起，
    真正安慰全来自你，
    你会赐我勇气。
    原本将灭的希望之光，
    又重新点燃绽放。
    内心再次振作坚强，
    将你圣名传讲。
    你是我的依靠、希望和力量，
    我的需要你不遗忘。
    满怀信心将真理向人宣扬，
    因你时刻在我身旁。
