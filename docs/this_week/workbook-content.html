效法爱的完美典范


1．耶和华上帝，爱的完美典范，
教导人表现爱。
从他的作为我们就能明白，
上帝的爱无处不在。
耶和华的爱流传千秋万代，
他宽恕过犯，甚至牺牲最爱，
献出他儿子把人的罪遮盖，
这就是爱，上帝的真爱。
2．要效法上帝，表现真挚爱心，
对彼此显温情。
我们要关心所有弟兄姐妹，
不分你我，开阔心胸。
我们爱上帝，也爱弟兄团体，
要体现爱心，做得更加彻底，
别人的过犯，我们包容宽待，
这就是爱，弟兄的真爱。
3．用爱心维系弟兄姐妹友谊，
一家人心连心。
仁爱的天父亲切地邀请人，
一同感受团结气氛。
请加入我们，共享友爱欢乐，
让上帝力量重新塑造我们。
愿彼此激励，就不轻易忘记：
这就是爱，上帝就是爱。

Can We Really Please God?

Job suffered a series of personal hardships that seemed unfair. He got the wrong idea, concluding that God did not care whether he kept his faith in Him or not. (Job 9:20-22) Job was so convinced of his own righteousness that to others it sounded as though he were proclaiming himself more righteous than God.​—Job 32:1, 2; 35:1, 2.

What Does Jehovah’s Loyal Love Mean to You?

14. How did David describe the way God’s loyal love protects us?

14 God’s loyal love provides spiritual protection. In prayer to Jehovah, David stated: “You are a hiding place for me; you will safeguard me from distress. You will surround me with joyful shouts of deliverance. . . . The one trusting in Jehovah is surrounded by His loyal love.” (Ps. 32:7, 10) Just as the walls surrounding an ancient city protected its citizens, so Jehovah’s acts of loyal love surround us, providing spiritual protection from dangers that could test our integrity. Further, Jehovah’s loyal love moves him to draw us to him.​—Jer. 31:3.

谁能知道耶和华的想法？

19 关于“耶和华的想法”，我们学得什么教益呢？要让上帝的话语帮助我们正确地明白耶和华的想法，不要用自己狭窄的观点、不完美的标准和想法去判断耶和华。约伯说：“［上帝］不像我是个人，让我可以回答他，又让我们可以彼此争讼。”（约伯记9:32）要是我们渐渐明白耶和华的想法，就会像约伯一样不禁赞叹说：“看哪！这不过是上帝作为的边缘。关于他，我们听见的，是多么微弱的声音！这样，他雷霆般的大能，谁能了解呢？”（约伯记26:14）

20．要是我们在圣经里读到一些难明的记载，就该怎么做？

20 我们在圣经里要是读到一些难明的记载，令我们对耶和华的想法尤其感到费解，那该怎么做呢？要是经过仔细查考以后，仍然找不着清晰的答案，就可以视之为一个考验，看看自己对耶和华有多信赖。要记住，有时我们对圣经里某些记载可能会感到费解，这时我们就有机会表明自己对耶和华的特质有深刻的认识，而且确信他的行动必定会反映这些特质。要谦卑地承认我们不可能明白上帝所做的每一件事。（传道书11:5）我们有这种态度，就会很认同使徒保罗以下的话：“上帝的厚恩、智慧、知识多么高深！他的判决多难测透！他的道路多难追寻！‘谁知道耶和华的想法？谁做过他的谋士呢？’‘谁先给了他，以至他非回报不可呢？’因为一切都是由他而来，靠他而有，为他而立。愿荣耀永远归于他。阿们。”（罗马书11:33-36）


 
[w10 10/15 6-7 ¶19-20]
“Who Has Come to Know the Mind of Jehovah?”

19 What have we learned about “the mind of Jehovah”? We need to allow God’s Word to mold our understanding of Jehovah’s mind. We should never impose our limitations on Jehovah and judge him by our standards and thinking. Job stated: “[God] is not a man like me that I should answer him, that we should come together in judgment.” (Job 9:32) Like Job, when we start to understand the mind of Jehovah, we cannot help but exclaim: “Look! These are the fringes of his ways, and what a whisper of a matter has been heard of him! But of his mighty thunder who can show an understanding?”​—Job 26:14.

20. What should we do if we come across a passage of Scripture that is difficult for us to understand?

20 As we read the Scriptures, what should we do if we come across a passage that is difficult to understand, especially with regard to Jehovah’s thinking? If after researching the matter we still do not have a clear answer, we can view this as a test of our trust in Jehovah. Remember, at times certain statements allow us an opportunity to express our faith in Jehovah’s qualities. Let us humbly acknowledge that we do not understand everything that he does. (Eccl. 11:5) We will thus be prompted to agree with these words of the apostle Paul: “O the depth of God’s riches and wisdom and knowledge! How unsearchable his judgments are and past tracing out his ways are! For ‘who has come to know Jehovah’s mind, or who has become his counselor?’ Or, ‘Who has first given to him, so that it must be repaid to him?’ Because from him and by him and for him are all things. To him be the glory forever. Amen.”​—Rom. 11:33-36.

上帝的王国将要成就什么事？

虽然耶稣已经开始在天上统治，但地球仍然受人类政府管辖。不过，上帝已吩咐耶稣：“你要去敌人当中征服他们。”（诗篇110:2）在不久的将来，耶稣就会铲除所有敌对势力，并为愿意服从上帝的人带来纾解。

上帝的王国很快就会采取什么行动呢？

  • 毁灭错误宗教 许多宗教教人错误的道理，令人误解上帝，也使人活得很痛苦。圣经把这些错误宗教比作“娼妓”，并预告她会被铲除，而许多人会因为她的灭亡感到震惊。（启示录17:15,16）

  • 终结人类的统治 上帝的王国会采取行动，终结人类的统治。（启示录19:15,17,18）

  • 铲除所有恶人 一心作恶、拒绝服从上帝的人又会有什么下场呢？圣经说：“邪恶的人会从地上被铲除。”（箴言2:22）

  • 消灭撒但和邪灵 撒但和邪灵将无法“再迷惑列国”。（启示录20:3,10）

那么，对接受上帝王国统治的人来说，这一切又意味着什么呢？

上帝的王国会为人类做些什么？

作为天上的君王，耶稣将要成就的事会远远超越历史上的任何统治者。从人类当中会有14万4000人被挑选出来，和耶稣一起统治。（启示录5:9,10；14:1,3）耶稣会确保上帝的旨意在地上实现。那么，上帝的王国会怎样造福地上的人呢？

  • 消除疾病和死亡 “没有居民会说：‘我病了’”，而且也“不再有死亡。”——以赛亚书33:24；启示录21:4

  • 带来真正的和平与安全 “你的儿女会饱享安宁”，他们会“坐在自己的葡萄树下和无花果树下，没有人会使他们担惊受怕”。——以赛亚书54:13；弥迦书4:4

  • 给人很有意义的工作 “我挑选的人会充分享受自己劳碌的成果。他们不会白白劳碌。”——以赛亚书65:22,23

  • 解决环境问题 “旷野和旱地会欢欣，荒原也会快乐，像番红花盛放。”——以赛亚书35:1

  • 教导人怎样做就能得到永远的生命 “他们必须认识你这位独一的真神，以及你派来的耶稣基督，才能得到永远的生命。”——约翰福音17:3

Picture description: 战斗机、坦克和导弹

You may wonder, ‘How can we​—a small band of disciples—​possibly bear witness to “the most distant part of the earth”?’ You may recall the warning Jesus gave on the night before his death: “A slave is not greater than his master. If they have persecuted me, they will also persecute you; if they have observed my word, they will also observe yours. But they will do all these things against you on account of my name, because they do not know the One who sent me.” (John 15:20, 21) Reflecting on those words, you might ask yourself, ‘How can I bear thorough witness in the face of such opposition and persecution?’


Picture description: Brothers John Barr and Theodore Jaracz of the Governing Body looking at a world map. Brother Barr holds the book “‘Bearing Thorough Witness’ About God’s Kingdom.”
 
 

Today, we face similar questions. Our commission as Jehovah’s Witnesses likewise requires that we bear thorough witness to “the most distant part of the earth,” to “people of all the nations.” (Matt. 28:19, 20) How can this work be accomplished, especially in view of the foretold opposition?


 

Acts of Apostles gives us a thrilling account of how the apostles and their fellow Christians in the first century C.E. were able, with Jehovah’s help, to fulfill their assignment. The publication that you are now reading is designed to help you examine that record and feel the excitement of the fast-moving events recorded therein. You will be amazed at the number of parallels that exist between God’s servants of the first century and his people today. You will see that these parallels involve not only the work we do but also the way we are organized to do that work. Reflecting on these similarities will no doubt fortify your belief that Jehovah God is continuing to direct the earthly part of his organization.


 

It is our hope and prayer that examining the book of Acts will strengthen your confidence that Jehovah will help you and that the power of his holy spirit will sustain you. May you thereby be encouraged to continue “bearing thorough witness” about God’s Kingdom and helping others get on the path to salvation.​—Acts 28:23; 1 Tim. 4:16.

“你们要去，使所有国族的人做我的门徒”

概览使徒行传，从中汲取教益

1-6．请举出耶和华见证人在不同情况下传道的例子。


 

在加纳，有个年轻的耶和华见证人叫丽贝卡，她把自己就读的学校看作她的传道地区。丽贝卡的书包里常常放着一些圣经书刊，休息时她就找机会向同学作见证。结果，有好几个同学都愿意经常跟她讨论圣经。


 

2 在非洲东岸附近的热带海岛马达加斯加，有两位先驱常常在烈日下步行约25公里，到一个偏僻的村庄去，帮助一些想认识真理的人学习圣经。


 

3 在巴拉圭，为了接触巴拉圭河和巴拉那河沿岸的居民，耶和华见证人建造了一艘船。协助这项工程的志愿人员来自15个国家。这艘船的排水量达45吨，最多可以让12个人在船上生活。有了这艘船，热心的传道员就能把王国好消息传到偏远的地区去。


 

4 在美国，接近北极的阿拉斯加每逢夏季都有许多游客，当地的耶和华见证人就把握这个有利时机向人传道。天气暖和起来，很多外国游客都会坐邮轮前来。当他们下船时，耶和华见证人早已在码头等候，要向他们展示印刷精美、不同语言的圣经书刊。阿拉斯加有一些村落十分偏远，于是耶和华见证人坐飞机去那些地方传道，结果阿留申人、阿塔巴斯坎人、钦西安人和特林吉特人都能听到王国的好消息。


 

5 住在美国得克萨斯州的拉里有个特别的传道地区，就是他所住的疗养院。拉里在一次意外中受伤，要用轮椅代步。尽管这样，他还是努力向人谈论上帝王国的信息，包括圣经给他的希望，就是当上帝的王国统治时他可以再次走路。（以赛亚书35:5,6）


 

6 在缅甸，一群耶和华见证人从曼德勒乘船去北部参加大会，行程需要三天。为了尽力传讲王国的好消息，他们带了很多圣经书刊，好分发给船上的乘客。船一停在某镇或某村，这群热心的传道员就下船，迅速走遍大街小巷分发书刊。这段时间里有一些新乘客登船，当传道员再次上船时，船上已是个“新地区”了。

7．崇拜耶和华的人用什么方法为上帝的王国作见证？他们的目标是什么？


 

7 上述几个弟兄姐妹的例子充分表明，在世界各地，热心崇拜耶和华的人正“为上帝的王国作彻底的见证”。（使徒行传28:23）他们挨家传道，在街上向人作见证，也通过书信和电话传讲真理。无论是乘坐公共汽车，在公园散步，还是在工作的小休时间，耶和华见证人都抓紧每个机会宣扬上帝的王国。他们采用的方法尽管不同，但目标都一样：哪里有人，就在哪里传讲王国好消息。（马太福音10:11）

CHAPTER 1

“Go . . . and Make Disciples”

Overview of Acts of Apostles and how it relates to our day

1-6. Relate an experience of Witnesses preaching under a wide variety of circumstances.


 

REBECCA, a young Witness of Jehovah in Ghana, regarded her school as her own preaching territory. She always had Bible-based literature in her schoolbag. During break time, she looked for opportunities to give a witness to fellow students. Rebecca started Bible studies with several of her classmates.


 

2 On the island of Madagascar, just off the east coast of Africa, two pioneers regularly walked some 15 miles (25 km) in the tropical heat to get to a remote village. There they conducted a number of Bible studies with interested ones.


 

3 To reach people living along the Paraguay and Paraná rivers, Witnesses in Paraguay together with volunteers from 15 other countries worked to build a riverboat. The 45-ton boat can accommodate up to 12 people. From this floating home, zealous Kingdom preachers spread the good news into areas that were otherwise inaccessible.


 

4 In the Far North, Witnesses in Alaska took advantage of a unique opportunity to preach during the summer tourist season. When warmer weather brought cruise ships loaded with visitors of many different nationalities, local Witnesses stationed themselves on the dock with an attractive display of Bible literature in a wide variety of languages. In that same region, an airplane proved invaluable in reaching isolated villages, thus allowing the good news to be spread to Aleut, Athabascan, Tsimshian, and Tlingit communities.


 

5 Larry, in Texas, U.S.A., had a special territory​—the nursing home in which he resided. Although confined to a wheelchair as a result of an accident, Larry kept busy. He shared with others the Kingdom message, including his Bible-based hope that under Kingdom rule he will one day walk again.​—Isa. 35:5, 6.


 

6 To attend an assembly in upper Myanmar, a group of Witnesses made a three-day journey by ferryboat from Mandalay. Eager to preach the good news, they took along Bible literature, which they offered to fellow passengers. Each time the ferry stopped at a town or a village, the energetic preachers got off and quickly went through the settlement, offering literature. In the meantime, new passengers boarded the ferry, becoming “fresh territory” for the returning Kingdom publishers.

7. In what ways do worshippers of Jehovah bear witness about God’s Kingdom, and what is their goal?


 

7 Like the brothers and sisters mentioned in these few examples, zealous worshippers of Jehovah around the world are “bearing thorough witness concerning the Kingdom of God.” (Acts 28:23) They call from house to house, approach people on the street, write them letters, and speak to them over the phone. Whether riding on a bus, walking in a park, or taking a break at their place of secular work, they eagerly look for every opportunity to give a witness about God’s Kingdom. The specific methods may vary, but the goal is the same​—preach the good news wherever people can be found.​—Matt. 10:11.
