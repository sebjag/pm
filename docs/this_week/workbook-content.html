Eliphaz Demonstrates How Not to Give Comfort

Eliphaz told Job that humans could never please God (Job 15:14-16; w05 9/15 26 ¶4-5)
 
[Job 15:14-16]
14 What is mortal man that he should be pure,

Or anyone born of a woman that he should be righteous?+

15 Look! He has no faith in his holy ones,

And even the heavens are not pure in his eyes.+

16 How much less so when a person is detestable and corrupt,+

A man who drinks in unrighteousness just like water!


 
[w05 9/15 26 ¶4-5]
Resist Wrong Thinking!

In all three speeches, Eliphaz presented the idea that God is so exacting that nothing his servants do is good enough for him. “Look! In his servants he has no faith,” Eliphaz told Job, “and his angels he charges with faultiness.” (Job 4:18, footnote) Eliphaz later said of God: “In his holy ones he has no faith, and the heavens themselves are actually not clean in his eyes.” (Job 15:15) And he asked: “Does the Almighty have any delight in that you are righteous?” (Job 22:3) Bildad was in agreement with this viewpoint, for he stated: “There is even the moon, and it is not bright; and the stars themselves have not proved clean in [God’s] eyes.”​—Job 25:5.

We must be on guard against being influenced by such thinking. It can lead us to feel that God requires too much of us. This view attacks our very relationship with Jehovah. Moreover, if we succumb to this type of reasoning, how would we respond when we are given needed discipline? Rather than humbly accepting the correction, our heart may become “enraged against Jehovah himself,” and we may harbor resentment toward him. (Proverbs 19:3) How spiritually disastrous that would be!


 

Eliphaz implied that Job was wicked and that this was why he was suffering (Job 15:20)
 
[Job 15:20]
20 A wicked person suffers torment all his days,

Throughout all the years reserved for the tyrant.

[Job 16:1, 2]

What Eliphaz said to Job was a lie. Jehovah values our efforts to serve him. (Ps 149:4) Even the righteous can expect to experience problems.​—Ps 34:19.
 
[Ps 149:4]
 4 For Jehovah takes pleasure in his people.+

He adorns the meek with salvation.+


 
[Ps 34:19]
19 Many are the hardships* (Footnote: Or “calamities.”) of the righteous one,+

But Jehovah rescues him from them all.+


 
 FOR MEDITATION: How can we “speak consolingly to those who are depressed”?​—1Th 5:14; w15 2/15 9 ¶16.
 
[1Th 5:14]
14 On the other hand, we urge you, brothers, to warn the disorderly,+ speak consolingly to those who are depressed,+ support the weak, be patient toward all.+

 
[w15 2/15 9 ¶16]
Imitate Jesus’ Humility and Tenderness

16. What can we say to encourage those who are depressed?

16 Our tender words. Our tender affection for others moves us to “speak consolingly to those who are depressed.” (1 Thess. 5:14) What can we say to encourage such ones? We can lift their spirits by expressing our genuine care and concern for them. We can offer sincere commendation to help them see their positive qualities and abilities. We can remind them that Jehovah drew them to his Son, so they must be precious in his eyes. (John 6:44) We can reassure them that Jehovah deeply cares for his servants who are “brokenhearted” or “crushed in spirit.” (Ps. 34:18) Our tender words can have a healing effect on those who need comfort.​—Prov. 16:24.

约伯记经文选讲

7:9,10；10:21；16:22 约伯这些话表示他不相信复活吗？ 约伯这几句话是就自己不久的未来而言。他的话是什么意思呢？意思也许是，如果他死了，跟他同年代的人就不会再看见他。从他们的角度来看，在上帝指定的时候来到之前，约伯再也不会回家，别人也不再认识他了。约伯的意思也可能是，人死后绝不能够凭自己的力量重得生命。事实上，约伯记14:13-15清楚表明，约伯相信死了的人有希望在未来复活。


 
[w06 3/15 14 ¶11]
Highlights From the Book of Job

7:9, 10; 10:21; 16:22​—Do these statements indicate that Job did not believe in the resurrection? These are comments about Job’s immediate future. What, then, did he mean? One possibility is that if he should die, none of his contemporaries would see him. From their standpoint, he would neither return to his house nor get further acknowledgment until God’s appointed time. Job might also have meant that no one can come back from Sheol on his own. That Job hoped in a future resurrection is clear from Job 14:13-15.

流露温情，感同身受


 
Picture description: 经文
 

帖撒罗尼迦前书2:7,8


 

摘要：说话时要流露真情，让听者感受到你关心他们。


 

要诀：

  • 将心比心 想想听者可能遇到的困难或烦恼，试着体会他们的感受。

  • 留意用词 设法鼓励和安慰听者，并使他们重新振作。小心挑选用词，以免在无意间冒犯听者。不要用轻蔑的口吻谈论未信主的人或他们的宗教信仰。

  • 流露感情 仁慈的语气和适当的手势可以显出你对听者的关心。要留意你的表情，保持笑容。


     
    Picture description: 实用建议
     

    Box content: 表达情感时不该做作或夸张。朗读时要传达情绪，但要避免让听者把焦点放在你身上。通常，发音时强调辅音（子音）会让人觉得刺耳、不友善；强调元音（母音）会让你的声音听起来亲切悦耳。

    第8课

    比喻有教导作用


     
    Picture description: 经文
     

    马太福音13:34,35


     

    摘要：善用比喻提升教导技巧。用简单的比喻引起听者共鸣并说明要点。


     

    要诀：

    • 用简单的事物打比喻 我们可以学习耶稣的教导方式，他说话深入浅出，以小喻大。比喻要跟论点吻合，不要在比喻里加添无关的细节，这会让听者看不出比喻和论点的关系，使他们分心。


       
      Picture description: 实用建议
       

      Box content: 要多观察，留意周遭的事物，研读圣经书刊，并且向教导经验丰富的传道员学习。把比喻记下来并保存好。善用这些比喻可以提升你的教导能力。

    • 从听者的角度选用比喻 选用跟听者有关而且能引起共鸣的比喻。小心运用比喻，你的比喻不该让听者觉得尴尬或被冒犯。

    • 用比喻施行教导，解释要点 要用比喻强调要点，而非解释细节。要让听者记得论点，而不是只记得比喻。

    第2章

    你们会“做我的见证人”

    耶稣怎样帮助使徒准备好推广传道工作

    根据使徒行传1:1-26

    1-3．耶稣是怎样离开使徒的？哪些问题值得我们想想？


     

    耶稣复活后，使徒们愁云尽散，雀跃不已。这40天来，耶稣一再显现，进一步教导他们，鼓励他们。过去的几个星期，使徒们多么兴奋！他们真希望这一切不会结束！但今天是耶稣最后一次显现了。


     

    2 使徒一起站在橄榄山上，仔细聆听耶稣的每一句话。耶稣说完了，使徒却意犹未尽。此时，耶稣举手给他们祝福。接着，耶稣开始从地面升起来！使徒目送他升上天空，后来有云彩遮住了他，再也看不见了。耶稣已经走了，但他们仍旧凝视着天空。（路加福音24:50；使徒行传1:9,10）


     

    3 对耶稣的使徒来说，看见这一幕是他们的人生转折点。他们的主耶稣基督已经升天了，他们该怎么办呢？我们可以肯定，耶稣已经帮助他们做好准备，让他们能继续从事他所开始的工作。那么，耶稣怎样帮助使徒做好准备去执行这项重要任务呢？对于这项任务，使徒有什么态度？这跟今天的基督徒有什么关系？使徒行传第1章回答了这些问题，答案会让我们深受鼓励。


    Picture description: 使徒们看着耶稣升到天上去。
     
     

    “许多令人信服的证据”（使徒行传1:1-5）

    4．路加在使徒行传的起头怎样说？


     

    4 在使徒行传里，路加一开始就表明这卷书是写给提阿非罗的，而他之前写成的路加福音也是写给这个人的。a为了表明使徒行传是路加福音的续篇，路加在使徒行传的开头重提路加福音结尾记述的事，只是字眼略有不同，又补上了一些细节而已。

    5,6．（甲）什么能使耶稣的门徒保持坚强的信心？（乙）为什么说，今天基督徒的信心基于确凿的证据？


     

    5 什么能使耶稣的门徒保持坚强的信心？使徒行传1:3说：“［耶稣］用许多令人信服的证据，向他们证明自己真的活过来了。”在圣经的原文里，译作“令人信服的证据”的希腊语词，只有“亲爱的医生”路加用过。（歌罗西书4:14）这个词是个术语，常在医学着作中用来指无可置疑的证据。耶稣提供了确凿的证据。他曾多次向门徒显现，有时是向一两个门徒显现，有时是向所有使徒显现，还有一次是向五百多个信徒显现。（哥林多前书15:3-6）这些证据都是确凿无疑的！


     

    6 今天，真基督徒的信心也是以许多确凿的证据为基础的。有没有证据证明耶稣曾在地上活过，为我们的罪而死，之后又复活呢？当然有！上帝的话语记载了目击者的可靠陈述，提供了充分而令人信服的证据。我们研读这些记载，并祈求上帝的指导，信心就会更坚定。要记住，跟轻信不同，真信心是以可靠的证据为基础的。要得到永生，一定要有真信心才行。（约翰福音3:16）

    7．耶稣在教导和传道方面为门徒树立了什么榜样？


     

    7 耶稣还“谈论上帝的王国”。比如说，他向门徒解释关于弥赛亚必须受难和死亡的预言。（路加福音24:13-32,46,47）当耶稣表明自己就是弥赛亚时，他正是在强调上帝的王国这个主题，因为他就是上帝委任的君王。耶稣传道的主题一向都是上帝的王国，因此今天他的门徒传道也以上帝的王国为主题。（马太福音24:14；路加福音4:43）

    CHAPTER 2

    “You Will Be Witnesses of Me”

    How Jesus prepared his apostles to spearhead the preaching work

    Based on Acts 1:1-26

    1-3. How does Jesus part from his apostles, and what questions arise?


     

    THEY do not want it to end. To the apostles, the past weeks have been thrilling! The resurrection of Jesus lifted them from the depths of despair to the heights of joy. For 40 days now, Jesus has appeared repeatedly, further teaching and encouraging his followers. This day, however, he is appearing for the last time.


     

    2 Standing together on the Mount of Olives, the apostles hang on Jesus’ every word. When he finishes​—all too soon, it seems—​he lifts his hands and blesses them. Then, he begins to rise from the earth! His followers gaze after him as he ascends into the sky. Finally, a cloud hides him from their view. He is gone, but they keep staring into the heavens.​—Luke 24:50; Acts 1:9, 10.


     

    3 This scene marks a turning point in the life of Jesus’ apostles. What will they do now that their Master, Jesus Christ, has ascended to heaven? Rest assured, their Master has prepared them to take up the work he began. How did he equip them for this important assignment, and how did they respond? And how are Christians today affected? The first chapter of Acts contains the encouraging answers.


    Picture description: Jesus ascending to heaven as the apostles gaze up at him.
     
     

    “Many Convincing Proofs” (Acts 1:1-5)

    4. How does Luke open his account recorded in the book of Acts?


     

    4 Luke begins his account by addressing Theophilus, the same man to whom he earlier wrote his Gospel.a Making it clear that this record is a continuation of the first, Luke begins by summarizing the events recorded at the end of his Gospel, using different wording and providing some fresh detail.

    5, 6. (a) What will help Jesus’ followers to keep their faith strong? (b) How is the faith of Christians today based on “many convincing proofs”?


     

    5 What will keep the faith of Jesus’ followers strong? At Acts 1:3, we read of Jesus: “He showed himself alive to them by many convincing proofs.” In the Bible, only “the beloved physician” Luke used the word rendered “convincing proofs.” (Col. 4:14) It was a term used in technical medical writings, and it signifies evidence that is demonstrative, conclusive, reliable. Jesus furnished such evidence. He appeared to his followers many times, sometimes to one or two, sometimes to all the apostles, and on one occasion to more than 500 believers. (1 Cor. 15:3-6) Convincing proofs indeed!


     

    6 The faith of true Christians today is likewise based on “many convincing proofs.” Is there evidence that Jesus lived on earth, died for our sins, and was raised up? Absolutely! Reliable eyewitness accounts in God’s inspired Word provide all the convincing evidence we need. Studying these accounts prayerfully can greatly strengthen our faith. Remember, solid evidence can make the difference between genuine faith and mere credulity. Real faith is essential to gaining everlasting life.​—John 3:16.

    7. Jesus set what example for his followers in teaching and preaching?


     

    7 Jesus was also “speaking about the Kingdom of God.” For example, he explained prophecies that showed that the Messiah would have to suffer and die. (Luke 24:13-32, 46, 47) When Jesus clarified his role as the Messiah, he stressed the theme of God’s Kingdom, for he was King-Designate. The Kingdom was always the theme of Jesus’ preaching, and his followers today stick to the same theme as they preach.​—Matt. 24:14; Luke 4:43.
