全力支持正确的宗教

耶和华会怎样记念你？

事实上，圣经自始至终表明，对上帝来说，“记念”含有采取积极行动的意思。例如，大地被洪水淹没150日之后，“上帝记念挪亚”，“叫风吹地，水势渐落”。（创世记8:1）多个世纪之后，参孙被非利士人用链子锁住，双目失明。他恳求上帝说：“主耶和华啊，求你眷念我。上帝啊，求你赐我这一次的力量。”耶和华垂听参孙的祷告，记念他，赐他超凡的气力，让他可以在上帝的仇敌身上施行报复。（士师记16:28-30）同样，耶和华应允了尼希米的呼求，祝福他的努力，使纯真崇拜得以在耶路撒冷复兴过来。


 
 尼希米是省长，却没有期待得到什么特殊的待遇。他为会众里有职责的人或在特别岗位服务的人树立了很好的榜样。

 TREASURES FROM GOD’S WORD
 

Nehemiah Wanted to Serve, Not Be Served

Nehemiah did not use his authority for selfish gain (Ne 5:14, 15, 17, 18; w02 11/1 27 ¶3)
 
[Ne 5:14, 15,]
14 Moreover, from the day that he commissioned me to become their governor+ in the land of Judah, from the 20th year+ to the 32nd year+ of King Ar·ta·xerxʹes,+ 12 years, neither I nor my brothers ate the food allowance due the governor.+ 15 But the former governors who were before me had burdened the people and had been taking from them 40 silver shekels* (Footnote: A shekel equaled 11.4 g (0.367 oz t). See App. B14.) for bread and wine each day. Also, their attendants had oppressed the people. But I did not do that+ because of the fear of God.+

 
[ 17, 18]
17 There were 150 Jews and deputy rulers dining at my table, as well as those who came to us from the nations. 18 Every day one bull, six choice sheep, and birds were prepared for me,* (Footnote: Or “at my expense.”) and once every ten days we had all sorts of wine in abundance. Despite all this I did not demand the food allowance due the governor, because the people were already bearing their burden of service.

 
[w02 11/1 27 ¶3]
Supporters of True Worship—Then and Now

Nehemiah contributed more than his time and organizational skills. He also used his material means to support true worship. He used his own money to buy back his Jewish brothers from slavery. He lent money without interest. He never “made it heavy” upon the Jews by demanding an allowance as governor, something to which he was entitled. Instead, he kept an open house to feed “a hundred and fifty men, and those coming in to us from the nations that were around us.” Each day he provided “one bull, six select sheep and birds” for his guests. In addition, once every ten days he offered them “every sort of wine in abundance”​—all at his own expense.​—Nehemiah 5:8, 10, 14-18.


 

Nehemiah did not just oversee the work; he also participated in it (Ne 5:16; w16.09 6 ¶16)
 
[Ne 5:16]
16 Furthermore, I took a hand in the work on this wall, and not a field did we acquire;+ all my attendants were gathered there for the work.

 
[w16.09 6 ¶16]
“Do Not Let Your Hands Drop Down”

16. Like Nehemiah, how can elders strengthen the hands of those in the congregation? Give examples of how fellow believers have helped you personally.

16 With Jehovah’s backing, Nehemiah and those with him strengthened their hands for the work. They then completed Jerusalem’s walls in just 52 days! (Neh. 2:18, ftn.; 6:15, 16) Nehemiah did not simply oversee the work. He was personally involved in rebuilding Jerusalem’s walls. (Neh. 5:16) Similarly, many loving elders have been able to imitate Nehemiah by assisting on theocratic construction projects or by cleaning and maintaining their local Kingdom Hall. By working along with fellow publishers in the ministry and by making shepherding visits, they also strengthen the weak hands of those who are anxious at heart.​—Read Isaiah 35:3, 4.


 

Nehemiah asked Jehovah to remember him for his self-sacrificing love (Ne 5:19; w00 2/1 32)
 
[Ne 5:19]
19 Do remember me favorably,* (Footnote: Or “for good.”) O my God, for all that I have done in behalf of this people.+

 
[w00 2/1 32]
How Will Jehovah Remember You?

Consistently, the Bible shows that with God, “remembering” means taking positive action. For example, after the earth had been inundated by the floodwaters for 150 days, “God remembered Noah . . . , and God caused a wind to pass over the earth, and the waters began to subside.” (Genesis 8:1) Centuries later, Samson, blinded and chained by the Philistines, prayed: “Jehovah, remember me, please, and strengthen me, please, just this once.” Jehovah remembered Samson by giving him superhuman strength so that he could avenge himself against God’s enemies. (Judges 16:28-30) As for Nehemiah, Jehovah blessed his efforts, and true worship was restored in Jerusalem.


 
 Although Nehemiah was the governor, he did not expect special treatment. He is a good example for those who have privileges and responsibilities in the congregation.

15 第三，敌人收买以色列人示玛雅，叫他诱惑尼希米违犯上帝的律法。示玛雅对尼希米说：“我们相约在上帝的家里，在圣殿里见面吧。我们还要关上殿门，因为有人要来杀你”。示玛雅声称，有人要暗杀尼希米，尼希米想要保全性命，就得躲藏在圣殿里。但尼希米并不是祭司，擅自躲进上帝的家里，就是犯罪。他会违犯上帝的律法，以求保全性命吗？尼希米回答说：“像我这样的人，哪能闯进圣殿而不用死亡呢？我不进去！”尼希米并没有落入敌人的圈套。为什么？因为尼希米知道，示玛雅虽是同胞，却不是上帝差来的。如果示玛雅是真先知，又怎会叫他违犯上帝的律法呢？再一次，尼希米并没有让自己被恶敌所胜。没过多久，他报告说：“以禄月二十五日，城墙终于完工，一共修建了五十二天。”（尼希米记6:10-15；民数记1:51；18:7）


 
[w07 7/1 30 ¶15]
“Keep Conquering the Evil With the Good”

15 Third, Nehemiah’s enemies used a traitor, the Israelite Shemaiah, to try to make Nehemiah break God’s Law. Shemaiah said to Nehemiah: “Let us meet by appointment at the house of the true God, within the temple, and let us close the doors of the temple; for they are coming in to kill you.” Shemaiah said that Nehemiah was about to be assassinated but that he could save his life by hiding in the temple. However, Nehemiah was not a priest. He would commit a sin by hiding in the house of God. Would he break God’s Law in an attempt to save his life? Nehemiah responded: “Who is there like me that could enter into the temple and live? I shall not enter!” Why did Nehemiah not fall into the trap that had been set for him? Because he knew that although Shemaiah was a fellow Israelite, “it was not God that had sent him.” After all, a true prophet would never advise him to break God’s Law. Again, Nehemiah did not let himself be conquered by evil opposers. Shortly thereafter he could report: “At length the wall came to completion on the twenty-fifth day of Elul, in fifty-two days.”​—Nehemiah 6:10-15; Numbers 1:51; 18:7.

第2课

语气自然


 
Picture description: 经文
 

哥林多后书2:17


 

摘要：用真诚自然的语气表达你的想法，让听者感受到你关心他们。


 

要诀：

  • 祷告并做好准备 祷告求上帝帮助你把注意力放在要说的内容上。不要过度在意自己的表现，牢记你要说的要点，用自己的话表达，但不要把想说的话一字一句地背下来。


     
    Picture description: 实用建议
     

    Box content: 朗读经文或出版物的内容前，要熟悉资料，这样才会读得流畅。读出引述的话时，要语带感情，但不要太夸张。

  • 发自内心 想想对方为什么需要听到这个信息，这样你的肢体动作和面部表情就会流露诚意，让人感到亲切。


     
    Picture description: 实用建议
     

    Box content: 说话自然不代表你的态度可以轻率。咬字清晰、语法正确，别人才会认真听你要说的话。

  • 说话时看着听者 如果这么做不会冒犯人，尽量跟对方保持目光交流。发表演讲时不要扫视听众，要跟不同的人有目光接触，一次只看着一个人说话。

圣经说的真的能相信吗？

能，至少有两个理由：

  • 上帝痛恨苦难和不公正的事。在古代，崇拜上帝的人受到恶待时，耶和华上帝有什么感觉呢？圣经说，他看到他们“被人压迫欺凌”，就感到伤心难过。（士师记2:18）

  • 上帝关心我们每一个人。不是只有受苦的人“知道自己的忧伤和痛苦”，耶和华也清楚知道。（历代志下6:29,30）

选用合适的视觉教材


 
Picture description: 经文
 

创世记15:5


 

摘要：运用视觉教材使要点更鲜明。


 

要诀：

  • 选用可以提升教学品质的视觉教材 你可以用图片、图表、地图、时间轴或其他视觉教材来强调重点。不要用视觉教材来强调细节。要让听者记得要点，而不是只记得视觉教材。

  • 做妥准备，确保对方看见你准备的教材


     
    Picture description: 实用建议
     

    Box content: 上台前或传道前，要确保你的视觉教材已经准备好了。

17,18．我们跟同工一起传道时，可以怎样互相帮助？

17 耶稣差门徒两个两个地去传道。（马可福音6:7；路加福音10:1）使徒保罗提到一些同工，说他们一直跟他“并肩奋斗，传扬好消息”。（腓立比书4:3）按照圣经的先例，上帝的组织在1953年开始安排有经验的传道员训练其他同工传道。

18 你跟基督徒同工一起传道时，可以怎样互相帮助呢？（请读哥林多前书3:6-9）同伴引用经文，你也要打开自己的圣经一起看。不论是同伴还是住户说话，你都要留心听。这样，同伴在应付异议方面遇到困难时，你就可以帮他一把了。（传道书4:12）但有一点要注意：如果同伴正在跟住户推理，你就不该打断他的话。你过度热心可能会令同伴泄气，令住户摸不着头脑。有时候，插一两句话也许是恰当的，但不要长篇大论，要让同伴继续按他的思路推理。


 
 

19．我们应该谨记什么？为什么？

19 从一家走到下一家时，你和同伴也可以互相帮助。不妨讲讲怎样传道更能引起住户的兴趣。谈到传道地区的反应时，要避免说消极的话。此外，也不要议论其他同工的缺点。（箴言18:24）我们应该谨记自己不过是瓦器，传讲好消息的职务是珍宝。耶和华把这个珍宝交给我们，是极大的恩典。（请读哥林多后书4:1,7）让我们尽心竭力地做好传福音者的工作，表明自己珍视上帝所赐的珍宝。

Fulfill Your Role as an Evangelizer

17, 18. When accompanying someone in the ministry, how can we work as a team?

17 Jesus sent his disciples out in the preaching work by twos. (Mark 6:7; Luke 10:1) Later, the apostle Paul mentioned “fellow workers” who had “striven side by side with [him] in the good news.” (Phil. 4:3) In harmony with that Scriptural precedent, in 1953, Kingdom publishers began a program of training others in the ministry.

18 When you accompany another Christian in the ministry, how can you work as a team? (Read 1 Corinthians 3:6-9.) In your Bible, look up the scriptures that your partner is using. Keep your attention turned to your partner and to the householder when either of them is talking. Follow the discussion closely in case there is a need for you to assist your partner to overcome an objection. (Eccl. 4:12) A word of caution, though: Resist the urge to interrupt your partner when he or she is using an effective line of reasoning. Your unrestrained enthusiasm might discourage your partner and confuse the householder. At times, it may be appropriate to join in the discussion. But if you choose to say something, limit yourself to a brief comment or two. Then allow your partner to resume the lead.


 
 

19. What do we do well to remember, and why?

19 How can you and your partner assist each other while walking from door to door? Why not use the time to discuss ways that your presentation might be improved? Be careful that your comments about those living in your territory are not discouraging. Likewise, avoid the trap of harping on the negative traits of fellow evangelizers. (Prov. 18:24) We do well to remember that we are earthen vessels. Jehovah has shown us extraordinary kindness by entrusting us with the treasure of the ministry of the good news. (Read 2 Corinthians 4:1, 7.) So let all of us show appreciation for that treasure by doing our best to fulfill our role as evangelizers.

STUDY 20

Effective Conclusion


 
Picture description: Cited scripture
 

Ecclesiastes 12:13, 14


 

SUMMARY: In your final remarks, appeal to your listeners to accept and apply what they have learned.


 

HOW TO DO IT:

  • Connect your conclusion to your overall topic. Restate or rephrase your main points and theme.

  • Motivate your listeners. Show your listeners what to do, and give sound reasons for doing so. Speak earnestly and with conviction.

  • Keep your conclusion simple and brief. Do not introduce new main points. Using as few words as are needed, make a final appeal for action.


     
    Picture description: Practical tip
     

    Box content: Do not rush your conclusion, and do not let your voice simply fade away. Deliver the last few sentences with a note of finality.


 
Box title: IN THE MINISTRY
 

Box content: As you conclude the discussion, repeat the main point you want your listener to remember. If a conversation ends abruptly, conclude the conversation on a positive note. Even if a person is rude, respond in a way that may motivate him to listen next time.

选择适当的娱乐令耶和华高兴

耶和华是“快乐的上帝”。（提摩太前书1:11）他希望我们开开心心地享受生活，也希望我们在工作之余，抽出时间好好休息。在这一课，我们会看看怎样运用空闲时间做有益身心的事，同时也让耶和华高兴。

1．我们在选择娱乐时要记得什么？

你在空闲的时候喜欢做什么呢？有些人喜欢安静地待在家里读书、上网、听音乐、看电影。还有些人喜欢和朋友出去动一动，比如爬山、游泳、打球。不管我们喜欢做什么，都要好好判断自己选择的娱乐是不是“主所接纳的”。（以弗所书5:10）这样做很重要，因为现在流行的很多娱乐都跟耶和华憎恨的事情有关，比如暴力、通灵或不道德的性行为。（请读诗篇11:5）什么能帮助我们做明智的决定，选择适当的娱乐呢？

如果我们跟爱耶和华的人做朋友，就能受到好的影响，更容易在娱乐方面做明智的决定。我们在第48课学到“与有智慧的人同行，就有智慧”。相反，我们跟不爱上帝标准的人经常来往“就会吃亏”。（箴言13:20）

2．为什么控制娱乐的时间很重要？

就算某个娱乐不会影响我们跟耶和华的关系，我们也不要花太多时间，不然就没有足够的时间做更重要的事。圣经提醒我们“要尽力善用时间”。（请读以弗所书5:15,16）

LESSON 53

Choose Entertainment That Pleases Jehovah

Jehovah is “the happy God.” (1 Timothy 1:11) He wants us to be happy and to enjoy our life. He is pleased when we set aside time from work to relax. In this lesson, we will see how we can use our leisure time in ways that are enjoyable and satisfying​—and that also please Jehovah.

1. What should we remember when choosing entertainment?

What do you like to do when you relax? Some prefer to spend their leisure time quietly at home​—reading a book, listening to music, watching a movie, or browsing the Internet. Others enjoy being more active with friends​—hiking, swimming, or playing a game. Whatever our preference is, we need to make sure that our choice of entertainment is “acceptable to the Lord.” (Ephesians 5:10) It is important that we understand this because a lot of popular entertainment features things that Jehovah hates, such as violence, sexual immorality, or spiritism. (Read Psalm 11:5.) What will help us to make wise choices in our entertainment?

If we choose friends who love Jehovah, they can have a good influence on us and on our choice of entertainment. As we learned in a previous lesson, “the one walking with the wise will become wise.” On the other hand, if we regularly associate with people who have no love for God’s standards, we “will fare badly.”​—Proverbs 13:20.

2. Why is it important to control how much time we spend on entertainment?

Even if the entertainment we choose is wholesome, we need to be careful not to spend too much time on it. Otherwise, we may not have enough time to spend on more important activities. The Bible encourages us to make “the best use of [our] time.”​—Read Ephesians 5:15, 16.
