我的依靠、希望和力量


1．耶和华你赐予的希望，
我们紧握不放。
渴望与所有人分享，
未来美好盼望。
但有时忧虑让人心慌，
喜乐被恐惧隐藏。
希望曾经照亮心房，
如今黯淡无光。
你是我的依靠、希望和力量，
我的需要你不遗忘。
满怀信心将真理向人宣扬，
因你时刻在我身旁。
2．耶和华当我陷入困境，
求你使我想起，
真正安慰全来自你，
你会赐我勇气。
原本将灭的希望之光，
又重新点燃绽放。
内心再次振作坚强，
将你圣名传讲。
你是我的依靠、希望和力量，
我的需要你不遗忘。
满怀信心将真理向人宣扬，
因你时刻在我身旁。

Rely On Jehovah​—When?

Asa relied on Jehovah to defeat a large army (2Ch 14:9-12; w21.03 5 ¶12)
 
[2Ch 14:9-12]
9 Later Zeʹrah the E·thi·oʹpi·an came against them with an army of 1,000,000 men and 300 chariots.+ When he reached Ma·reʹshah,+ 10 Aʹsa went out against him and they drew up in battle formation in the Valley of Zephʹa·thah at Ma·reʹshah. 11 Aʹsa then called to Jehovah his God+ and said: “O Jehovah, it does not matter to you whether those you help are many or have no power.+ Help us, O Jehovah our God, for we are relying* (Footnote: Lit., “we lean.”) on you,+ and in your name we have come against this crowd.+ O Jehovah, you are our God. Do not let mortal man prevail against you.”+

12 So Jehovah defeated the E·thi·oʹpi·ans before Aʹsa and before Judah, and the E·thi·oʹpi·ans fled.+


 
[w21.03 5 ¶12]
Young Men—How Can You Gain the Trust of Others?

12. What qualities did King Asa have when he began to rule?

12 As a young man, King Asa was humble and courageous. For example, when he succeeded his father, Abijah, he launched a campaign against idolatry. He also “told Judah to search for Jehovah the God of their forefathers and to observe the Law and the commandment.” (2 Chron. 14:1-7) And when Zerah the Ethiopian invaded Judah with 1,000,000 soldiers, Asa wisely turned to Jehovah for help, saying: “O Jehovah, it does not matter to you whether those you help are many or have no power. Help us, O Jehovah our God, for we are relying on you.” This beautiful expression shows how much confidence Asa had in Jehovah’s ability to save him and his people. Asa trusted in his heavenly Father, and “Jehovah defeated the Ethiopians.”​—2 Chron. 14:8-12.


 

Asa later counted on the Syrians to defeat a smaller army (2Ch 16:1-3; w21.03 5 ¶13)
 
[2Ch 16:1-3]
16 In the 36th year of the reign of Aʹsa, King Baʹa·sha+ of Israel came up against Judah and began to build up* (Footnote: Or “fortify; rebuild.”) Raʹmah+ to prevent anyone from going out or coming in to* (Footnote: Or “leaving or entering the territory of.”) King Aʹsa of Judah.+ 2 At that Aʹsa brought out silver and gold from the treasuries of Jehovah’s house+ and the king’s house* (Footnote: Or “palace.”) and sent them to King Ben-haʹdad of Syria,+ who was dwelling in Damascus, saying: 3 “There is a treaty* (Footnote: Or “covenant.”) between me and you and between my father and your father. I am sending you silver and gold. Come, break your treaty* (Footnote: Or “covenant.”) with King Baʹa·sha of Israel, so that he will withdraw from me.”

 
[w21.03 5 ¶13]
Young Men—How Can You Gain the Trust of Others?

13. What later happened to Asa, and why?

13 You would no doubt agree that facing an army of 1,000,000 soldiers was a huge challenge, and it was one that Asa met successfully. Sadly, however, when a less daunting challenge presented itself, Asa did not consult Jehovah. On being threatened by wicked King Baasha of Israel, Asa turned to the king of Syria for help. That decision led to disaster! Through his prophet Hanani, Jehovah told Asa: “Because you relied on the king of Syria and did not rely on Jehovah your God, the army of the king of Syria has escaped out of your hand.” In fact, from then on, Asa was constantly at war. (2 Chron. 16:7, 9; 1 Ki. 15:32) What is the lesson?


 

Jehovah was displeased with Asa for not continuing to seek His help (2Ch 16:7-9)
 
[2Ch 16:7-9]
7 At that time Ha·naʹni+ the seer came to King Aʹsa of Judah and said to him: “Because you relied* (Footnote: Lit., “leaned.”) on the king of Syria and did not rely* (Footnote: Lit., “lean.”) on Jehovah your God, the army of the king of Syria has escaped out of your hand.+ 8 Were not the E·thi·oʹpi·ans and the Libʹy·ans a very great army with many chariots and horsemen? But because you relied on Jehovah, he gave them into your hand.+ 9 For the eyes of Jehovah are roving about through all the earth+ to show his strength* (Footnote: Or “support.”) in behalf of those whose heart is complete toward* (Footnote: Or “completely devoted to.”) him.+ You have acted foolishly in this matter; from now on there will be wars against you.”+

 We may rely on Jehovah when making life-altering decisions, but what about at other times? It is important to take notice of Jehovah in all our ways.​—Pr 3:5, 6; w21.03 6 ¶14.
 
[Pr 3:5, 6]
 5 Trust in Jehovah+ with all your heart,

And do not rely* (Footnote: Lit., “lean.”) on your own understanding.+

 6 In all your ways take notice of him,+

And he will make your paths straight.+


 
[w21.03 6 ¶14]
Young Men—How Can You Gain the Trust of Others?

14. How can you rely on Jehovah, and according to 1 Timothy 4:12, what will you become if you do so?

14 Remain humble and continue to rely on Jehovah. When you got baptized, you showed great faith and trust in Jehovah. And Jehovah happily entrusted you with the privilege of becoming a member of his family. The key now is to continue to rely on Jehovah. It may seem that it is easy to rely on Jehovah when you must make life-altering decisions, but what about at other times? How important it is that you trust in Jehovah when making decisions, including those regarding entertainment, secular work, and life goals! Do not rely on your own wisdom. Instead, look for Bible principles that apply to your circumstances, and then act in harmony with that direction. (Prov. 3:5, 6) If you do so, you will make Jehovah happy and you will earn the respect of those in your congregation.​—Read 1 Timothy 4:12.

7,8．在事奉耶和华方面，你可以怎样效法亚撒？

7 每个人都可以省察自己，看看自己是否全心爱上帝。我们可以问问自己：“我决心服从耶和华，维护正确崇拜和会众的洁净吗？”请想想亚撒需要鼓起多大的勇气，才能废除玛迦“太皇太后”的身份！你认识的人当中可能没有人的行为像玛迦那样，但在某些情况下你可能要像亚撒一样勇敢。比如说，要是你的家人或好朋友犯了罪而且不愿意悔改，结果被开除了，你会采取果断的行动，不再跟那个人来往吗？你心里会想要怎么做呢？


 
[w17.03 19 ¶7]
Serve Jehovah With a Complete Heart!

7, 8. How can you imitate Asa in serving Jehovah?

7 Each of us can examine his heart to see if it is fully devoted to God. Ask yourself, ‘Am I determined to please Jehovah, to defend true worship, and to protect his people from any corrupting influence?’ Think how much courage Asa had to muster to stand up to Maacah, who was “queen mother” in the land! You probably do not know anyone who acts just like her, but there may be a situation in which you can imitate Asa’s zeal. For example, what if a member of your family or a close friend sins, is unrepentant, and has to be disfellowshipped? Would you take decisive action by ceasing to associate with that person? What would your heart move you to do?

提摩太前书4:13


 

摘要：将资料正确地朗读出来。


 

要诀：

  • 做好准备 想一想资料要传达的意思。练习把句子分成词组，一组一组地读出来，不要一个字一个字地读。避免漏字、加字、改字，也要留意标点符号。


     
    Picture description: 实用建议
     

    Box content: 请人听你朗读，并指出你读错的地方。

  • 口齿清晰 朗读时头要抬起来，嘴巴要张得够大，声音才会发得清楚。练习把每个音都完整地发出来。


     
    Picture description: 实用建议

有效的引言


 
Picture description: 经文
 

使徒行传17:22


 

摘要：引言该激发听者的兴趣，表明主题，也要让听者知道这个话题跟他有什么关系。


 

要诀：

  • 激发兴趣 你可以问一个问题、引录一段话、讲述真人真事，或找一则新闻作为引言，引发听者的兴趣。


     
    Picture description: 实用建议
     

    Box content: 考虑听者关心什么，对什么话题感兴趣，然后根据他们的情况修改引言。

  • 表明主题 告诉对方你要谈什么，以及为什么要谈这个话题。

  • 说明主题的重要性 根据听者的情况改变你要说的话，让他们知道这个主题跟自己有什么关系。


     
    Picture description: 实用建议
     

    Box content: 准备演讲时想一想：“会众里的弟兄姐妹需要的是什么？”然后根据他们的情况修改引言。


 
Box title: 传道建议
 

Box content: 展开交谈前，先观察那个人在做什么，也观察一下他身边的事物，推测他可能对什么话题感兴趣，然后根据你观察到的，简单地说一两句话或问对方一个问题。

热诚

罗马书12:11

清楚解释经文的意义


 
Picture description: 经文
 

约翰福音10:33-36


 

摘要：不要读完经文后就匆匆跳到下一个论点，要让对方清楚知道经文与论点之间的关联。


 

要诀：

  • 突出关键字眼 读出经文后，强调经文中跟主要论点有关的字眼。你可以复述关键字眼，或者提出问题，让听者留意到关键词。


     
    Picture description: 实用建议
     

    Box content: 如果你想要用不同的字眼代替经文中的关键词，还是要使听者看出经文跟要点之间有什么关系。

    Picture description: 实用建议
     

    Box content: 解释经文时不要合上圣经。这样听者就能看出你说的话有圣经根据。

  • 用浅显易懂的方式解释经文的意义 想想听者对这个主题的熟悉度，再决定要补充多少资料。要让论点清楚易懂，不要添加无关的细节。

Do All Celebrations Please God?

Jehovah wants us to enjoy life and even to celebrate at times. But do all celebrations and holidays please him? How can we show our love for Jehovah in this matter?

1. Why do many celebrations displease Jehovah?

You might be surprised to learn that many celebrations are based on unscriptural teachings or have pagan origins. Such celebrations may be connected with false religion. They may be linked to spiritism or be based on the idea that the soul is immortal. Some celebrations are rooted in superstition or a belief in luck. (Isaiah 65:11) Jehovah warns his worshippers: “Separate yourselves . . . and quit touching the unclean thing.”​—2 Corinthians 6:17.a

2. How does Jehovah feel about celebrations that give undue honor to humans?

Jehovah cautions us not to fall into the trap of putting our “trust in mere humans.” (Read Jeremiah 17:5.) Some holidays honor rulers or soldiers. Others celebrate national symbols or independence. (1 John 5:21) Still others promote political or social movements. How would Jehovah feel if we gave undue honor to a person or an organization, especially one that promoted ideas contrary to His purpose?

3. What behavior can make celebrations unacceptable?

The Bible condemns “overdrinking, wild parties, [and] drinking bouts.” (1 Peter 4:3) Some celebrations feature unrestrained and immoral behavior. To remain Jehovah’s friends, we need to stay far away from such moral uncleanness.


 

DIG DEEPER

4. Reject celebrations that dishonor Jehovah

Read Ephesians 5:10, and then discuss these questions:

  • What do we need to make sure of when deciding whether to celebrate a holiday?

  • What holidays are popular where you live?

  • Do you think those holidays please Jehovah?

For example, have you ever wondered what God thinks of birthday celebrations? The Bible never refers to a worshipper of Jehovah celebrating a birthday, but it does record two birthdays celebrated by people who did not serve Him. Read Genesis 40:20-22 and Matthew 14:6-10. Then discuss these questions:

  • What do both birthday celebrations have in common?

  • Based on these Bible accounts, how do you think Jehovah feels about birthdays?

Still, you may wonder, ‘Does Jehovah really care whether I participate in a birthday or other unscriptural celebration?’ Read Exodus 32:1-8. Then, play the VIDEO and discuss the questions that follow.

How to know if a celebration displeases God

  • Is it based on unscriptural teachings? To find out, investigate its origins.

  • Does it give undue honor to humans, organizations, or national symbols? We honor Jehovah above all and trust in him to solve the world’s problems.

  • Do its customs and practices conflict with Bible standards? We need to remain morally clean.

Endnotes

 

5. Holidays and Celebrations

Christians do not participate in holidays that displease Jehovah. But each Christian must use his Bible-trained conscience to decide how to handle certain situations regarding such holidays. Consider a few examples.

  • Someone wishes you a happy holiday. You may simply say, “Thank you.” If the person wants to learn more, you can explain why you do not celebrate the holiday.

  • Your marriage mate, who is not one of Jehovah’s Witnesses, invites you to have a meal with relatives during a holiday. If your conscience allows you to go, you could explain to your mate beforehand that if pagan customs are part of the meal, you will not participate in them.

  • Your employer offers you a bonus during the holiday season. Should you reject the bonus? Not necessarily. Does your employer view the bonus as part of the celebration, or does he view it as simply a way to show appreciation for your good work?

  • Someone gives you a gift during the holiday season. The gift-giver may say: “I know that you don’t celebrate the holiday, but I want you to have this.” Perhaps the person is just being kind. On the other hand, is there reason to think that he is trying to test your faith or involve you in celebrating the holiday? After considering this, it is up to you to decide whether to accept the gift. In all our decisions, we want to have a good conscience and be faithful to Jehovah.​—Acts 23:1.
